# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## General Questions

### What is Techsprint?
Techsprint is an intra-college hackathon where students form teams to solve real-world problems using technology within a limited timeframe.

### Who can participate?
Any currently enrolled student of our college can participate. You just need to form a team of 2-4 members.

### Do I need to be an expert coder?
No! While coding skills are helpful, we welcome participants of all skill levels. Hackathons are great learning opportunities.

### How much does it cost to participate?
Participation is free for all registered students.

---

## Registration & Teams

### How do I register?
Registration details will be shared through official college communication channels. Watch out for announcements!

### Can I participate alone?
Teams must have at least 2 members. We encourage teamwork and collaboration.

### Can I change my team after registration?
Team changes are generally not allowed after registration closes. Form your team carefully.

### What if my teammate drops out?
Contact the organizing team immediately. We'll try to help you find a replacement or merge with another team.

### Can I be part of multiple teams?
No, each participant can only be part of one team.

---

## Project Requirements

### What can I build?
Choose from the provided problem statements or propose your own innovative idea (in the Open Innovation category).

### Can I use code I wrote before the hackathon?
No, all code must be written during the hackathon. However, you can use existing libraries, frameworks, and APIs.

### What programming languages can I use?
Any! Use whatever languages and frameworks you're comfortable with.

### Can I use AI tools like ChatGPT or GitHub Copilot?
Yes, AI-assisted coding tools are allowed, but the project design and implementation decisions must be your team's own work.

### Do I need to build a complete product?
No, a working prototype that demonstrates your concept is sufficient. Focus on core functionality.

---

## Technical Questions

### Will internet be provided?
Yes, WiFi will be available throughout the event.

### What should I bring?
- Laptop/computer
- Chargers and adapters
- Any specific hardware for your project
- Student ID
- Enthusiasm and creativity!

### Can I use cloud services?
Yes, but make sure you can demo without relying on paid services. Use free tiers or demo modes.

### What if my project requires specific hardware?
Check with organizers beforehand. Limited hardware support may be available.

### Can I use external APIs?
Yes, you can use any public APIs. Make sure to respect API rate limits and terms of service.

## Event Logistics

### When is the hackathon?
Check the main README or official announcements for dates and timings.

### Where will it be held?
The venue will be announced closer to the event date.

### Will food be provided?
Yes, meals and refreshments will be provided during the event.

### Can I leave the venue and come back?
Check with organizers about the specific attendance policy for your event.

### What if I can't attend the entire event?
All team members should be present for the final presentation. Missing the presentation may lead to disqualification.

## Submission & Judging

### How do I submit my project?
Follow the guidelines in CONTRIBUTING.md. You'll submit your code via GitHub.

### What's the submission deadline?
The exact deadline will be announced during the event. Late submissions are not accepted.

### What happens if I miss the deadline?
Unfortunately, late submissions cannot be accepted due to fairness to other teams.

### How will projects be judged?
See [judging-criteria.md](judging-criteria.md) for detailed evaluation criteria.

### When will results be announced?
Results are typically announced at the closing ceremony on the final day.

### Can I ask judges questions during presentation?
Yes, there will be a Q&A session after your presentation.

## Prizes & Recognition

### What prizes can we win?
Cash prizes, certificates, goodies, and recognition! Specific prize details will be announced.

### Do all participants get certificates?
Yes, all participants receive certificates of participation.

### Are there special category awards?
Yes, in addition to top 3 positions, there may be awards for Best UI/UX, Best Innovation, etc.

## Code of Conduct

### What if someone is being disruptive?
Report immediately to the organizing team. We take the Code of Conduct seriously.

### What happens if rules are violated?
Depending on severity, consequences may include warnings or disqualification.

### Can I collaborate with other teams?
You can discuss ideas, but your code and implementation must be your team's original work.

## Post-Event

### Can I continue working on my project after the hackathon?
Absolutely! Many hackathon projects turn into real products. You own your code.

### Will projects be showcased?
Yes, winning projects may be featured on college platforms and social media.

### Can I add this to my resume?
Yes! Hackathon participation and wins are great resume additions.

### Will I get feedback on my project?
While detailed feedback may not be possible for all teams, judges often provide comments during Q&A.
