# Judging Criteria

## Overview

All projects will be evaluated by our panel of judges based on the following criteria. Understanding these criteria will help you focus your efforts on what matters most.

---

## Evaluation Categories

### 1. Innovation & Creativity (30 points)

**What we're looking for:**
- Uniqueness of the solution
- Creative approach to problem-solving
- Novel use of technology
- Originality of ideas

**Questions judges will ask:**
- Is this solution different from existing solutions?
- Does it show creative thinking?
- Is the approach innovative?

**Tips to score well:**
- Think outside the box
- Don't just replicate existing solutions
- Add unique features
- Show originality in your approach

---

### 2. Technical Implementation (25 points)

**What we're looking for:**
- Code quality and organization
- Proper use of technologies
- Technical complexity
- Best practices followed

**Questions judges will ask:**
- Is the code well-written and maintainable?
- Are appropriate technologies used?
- Does it demonstrate technical skill?
- Is the architecture sound?

**Tips to score well:**
- Write clean, commented code
- Follow coding standards
- Use modern frameworks and tools
- Demonstrate technical depth

---

### 3. Functionality (20 points)

**What we're looking for:**
- Working features
- Bug-free operation
- Completeness of implementation
- User experience

**Questions judges will ask:**
- Does the project work as intended?
- Are there any critical bugs?
- How complete is the implementation?
- Is it user-friendly?

**Tips to score well:**
- Test thoroughly before submission
- Focus on core features first
- Ensure demo-ready state
- Handle edge cases

---

### 4. Impact & Practicality (15 points)

**What we're looking for:**
- Real-world applicability
- Problem-solving effectiveness
- Potential for actual use
- Scalability

**Questions judges will ask:**
- Does this solve a real problem?
- Would people actually use this?
- Can this be scaled up?
- What's the potential impact?

**Tips to score well:**
- Choose relevant problems
- Demonstrate clear value
- Show use cases
- Explain potential impact

---

### 5. Presentation & Documentation (10 points)

**What we're looking for:**
- Clear communication
- Quality of demo
- Documentation completeness
- Professional presentation

**Questions judges will ask:**
- Can the team explain their project well?
- Is the documentation helpful?
- Is the demo convincing?
- How professional is the presentation?

**Tips to score well:**
- Practice your demo
- Create clear documentation
- Prepare for questions
- Be confident and clear

---

## Bonus Points (Up to 10 extra points)

### Bonus Categories:
- **Best Use of Specific Technology** (3 points)
- **Best UI/UX Design** (3 points)
- **Best Team Collaboration** (2 points)
- **Most Ambitious Project** (2 points)

---

## Scoring Scale

Each category is scored as follows:

- **Excellent (90-100%)**: Exceeds expectations
- **Good (75-89%)**: Meets all expectations
- **Satisfactory (60-74%)**: Meets most expectations
- **Needs Improvement (40-59%)**: Meets some expectations
- **Poor (0-39%)**: Does not meet expectations

---

## Final Scoring

**Total Points: 100**

| Rank | Score Range | Award |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 1st Place | 85-100 | Top Prize |
| 2nd Place | 75-84 | Second Prize |
| 3rd Place | 65-74 | Third Prize |
| Special Awards | Varies | Category-specific |

---

## Important Notes

1. **All criteria are equally important** - Don't neglect any category
2. **Judges' decisions are final** - Scores are not negotiable
3. **Plagiarism = Disqualification** - Must be original work
4. **Partial credit given** - Even incomplete projects can score well in some areas

---

## Judging Process

1. **Initial Review**: Code and documentation review
2. **Demo Session**: Live demonstration (5-7 minutes)
3. **Q&A Session**: Questions from judges (2-3 minutes)
4. **Deliberation**: Judges discuss and score
5. **Results**: Winners announced at closing ceremony

---

## Tips for Success

### Before the Hackathon:
- Understand the judging criteria
- Plan your project accordingly
- Allocate time for each aspect

### During the Hackathon:
- Balance all evaluation areas
- Don't over-focus on one aspect
- Keep track of time
- Test regularly

### For Presentation:
- Practice your demo multiple times
- Prepare for tough questions
- Highlight your strongest points
- Be honest about limitations

---

## Questions?

If you have questions about judging criteria, ask:
- Mentors during the event
- Organizing team via communication channel
- During the Q&A sessions
